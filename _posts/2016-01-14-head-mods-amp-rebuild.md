As mentioned all over the internet, if you had to focus your attention on one part of the Chevy inline 6 to gain power, it would be the cylinder head. The head was cast for stability and economy and requires significant modification and upgrades to get decent performance gains. 

The following is a list of things you can do to improve the Chevy 250 head for power:

* Install a lump port kit
* Increase valve size to 1.94/1.6 valves
* Complete a full intake and exhaust port job

With the ports opened and the lumps installed, it's time to lap the valves. Combustion chamber efficiency and overall compression will be affected by how well the valves are lapped. The process is simply a way of grinding one surface against another to create a flush mating area.

Since Turbo Camaro received a [performance camshaft](/posts/camshaft/){:target="_blank"}, new valve springs were also required. A set of [performance springs and retainers](http://www.12bolt.com/store/p12/Performance_Valve_spring%2F_retainer_kit.html){:target="_blank"} were picked up from 12bolt.com. The new springs handle up to .530 lift.
